1. QE Discretization Scheme for Heston Model¶

1.1. Notation declaration
  The Heston model is defined by the coupled two-dimensional SDE:
\begin{align*} &dS(t)/S(t)=\sqrt{V(t)}dW_S(t) ,\\ &dV(t)=\kappa(\theta-V(t))dt+\epsilon\sqrt{V(t)}dW_V(t), \end{align*}   where
  - $S$ and $V$ denote stock price and volatility, respectively
  - $W_S$ and $W_V$ are scalar Brownian motions in some probability measure and assume that $dW_S(t)\dot dW_V(t)=\rho dt$

  To discretize $V$, the modified Euler Scheme would take the form: \begin{align*} &\widehat{V}(t+\Delta)=\widehat{V}(t)+\kappa(\theta-\widehat{V}(t)^{+})\Delta+\epsilon\sqrt{\widehat{V}(t)^{+}}Z_V\sqrt{\Delta}, \end{align*}

  The modified Milstein Scheme would take the form: \begin{align*} &\widehat{V}(t+\Delta)=\widehat{V}(t)+\kappa(\theta-\widehat{V}(t)^{+})\Delta+\epsilon\sqrt{\widehat{V}(t)^{+}}Z_V\sqrt{\Delta}+\frac{\epsilon^2}{4}\Delta(Z_V^2-1), \end{align*}

1.2. TG Scheme
  Let \begin{align*} m&=\theta+(\widehat{V}(t)-\theta)e^{-\kappa\Delta},\tag{$1.1$}\\ s^2&=\frac{\widehat{V}(t)\epsilon^2 e^{-\kappa\Delta}}{\kappa}(1-e^{-\kappa\Delta})+\frac{\theta\epsilon^2}{2\kappa}(1-e^{-\kappa\Delta})^2,\tag{$1.2$}\\ \psi&\equiv s^2/m^2 =\frac{\frac{\widehat{V}(t)\epsilon^2 e^{-\kappa\Delta}}{\kappa}(1-e^{-\kappa\Delta})+\frac{\theta\epsilon^2}{2\kappa}(1-e^{-\kappa\Delta})^2}{(\theta+(\widehat{V}(t)-\theta)e^{-\kappa\Delta})^2}, \tag{$1.3$} \end{align*}   where the $m$ and $s^2$ are from the true conditional distribution, i.e. \begin{align*} &m\equiv E(V(t+\Delta)|V(t)=\widehat{V}(t)),\\ &s^2\equiv Var(V(t+\Delta)|V(t)=\widehat{V}(t)),\\ &\psi\equiv s^2/m^2>0, \end{align*}    The TG (Truncated Gaussian) Scheme writes $$\widehat{V}(t+\Delta)=(\mu+\sigma\dot Z_V)^{+},\tag{$1.4$}$$    where $Z_V$ is a standart Gaussian random variable, and $\mu$ and $\sigma$ are constants that will depend on the time-step $\Delta$ and $\widehat{V}(t)$, as well as the parameters in the SDE for $V$.
   Define the ratio $r=\mu/\sigma$, match the mean to $m$ results in: $$\mu=\frac{m}{r^{-1}\phi(r)+\Phi(r)};\quad \sigma=r^{-1}\mu=\frac{m}{\phi(r)+r\Phi(r)}$$    where $\phi$ is pdf of normal distribution and $\Phi$ is cdf of normal distribution.
   By matching moments and rearrangment, we have: $$r\phi(r)+\Phi(r)(1+r^2)=(1+\psi)(\phi(r)+r\Phi(r))^2$$    Then $r$ is only a function of $\psi$, i.e. $r=r(\psi)$ \begin{align*} &\mu=f_{\mu}(\psi)\cdot m,\quad f_{\mu}(\psi)=\frac{r(\psi)}{\phi(r(\psi))+r(\psi)\Phi(r(\psi))},\tag{$1.5$}\\ &\sigma=f_{\sigma}(\psi)\cdot s,\quad f_{\sigma}(\psi)=\frac{\psi^{-1/2}}{\phi(r(\psi))+r(\psi)\Phi(r(\psi))},\tag{$1.6$} \end{align*}   The detailed algorithm for the TG simulation step from $\widehat{V}(t)$ to $\widehat{V}(t+\Delta)$ is as follows:
  1.Given $\widehat{V}(t)$, compute $m$ and $s^2$ from (1.1) and (1.2)
  2.Compute $\psi=s^2/m^2$ and look up $f_{\mu}(\psi)$ and $f_{\sigma}(\psi)$ from the pre-calculated cache
  3.Compute $\mu$ and $\sigma$ according to equations (1.5) and (1.6)
  4.Compute $Z_V=\Phi^{-1}(U_V)$
  5.Set $\widehat{V}(t+\Delta)=(\mu+\sigma\dot Z_V)^{+}$

1.3. QE Scheme
   The non-central chi-square distribution approaches a Gaussian distribution as the non-centrality paremeter approaches $\infty$. However, for small $V(t)$, the non-centrality parameter approaches zero, and the Gaussian variable is typically not accurate.
   For sufficiently large values of $\widehat{V}(t)$, the QE (Quadratic Exponential) Scheme writes: $$\widehat{V}(t+\Delta)=a(b+Z_V)^2,\tag{$1.7$}$$    For low values of $\widehat{V}(t)$, we use asymptotic density and approximated density for $\widehat{V}(t+\Delta)$, we have the form: $$\Psi^{-1}(u)=\Psi^{-1}(u;p,\beta)=\left\{ \begin{aligned} &0, & 0\le u\le p \\ &\beta^{-1}ln(\frac{1-p}{1-u}),& p<u\le1. \end{aligned},\tag{$1.8$} \right. $$    where $\Psi(x)=Pr(\widehat{V}(t+\Delta)\leq x)=p+(1-p)(1-e^{-\beta x}), x\geq 0$ is the approximate cdf for low values of $\widehat{V}(t)$.
   The scheme varies when $\psi$ is big and when $\psi$ is small.
   Assume that some arbitrary level $\psi_c\in [1,2]$ has been selected. The detailed algorithm for the QE simulation step from $\widehat{V}(t)$ to $\widehat{V}(t+\Delta)$ is then:
  1. Given $\widehat{V}(t)$, compute $m$ and $s^2$ from the equations (1.1) and (1.2).
  2. Compute $\psi=s^2/m^2$
  3. Draw a uniform random number $U_V$
  4. If $\psi\leq \psi_c$:
    (a) Compute a and b as: \begin{align*} &b^2=2\psi^{-1}-1+\sqrt{2\psi^{-1}}\sqrt{{2\psi^{-1}-1}}\ge 0,\tag{$1.9$}\\ &a=\frac{m}{1+b^2},\tag{$1.10$}\\ \end{align*}     (b) Compute $Z_V=\Phi^{-1}(U_V)$
    (c) Set $\widehat{V}(t+\Delta)=a(b+Z_V)^2$
  5.Otherwise, if $\psi>\psi_c$:
    (a) Compute $\beta$ and $p$ as: \begin{align*} &p=\frac{\psi-1}{\psi+1}\in [0,1),\tag{$1.11$}\\ &\beta=\frac{1-p}{m}=\frac{2}{m(\psi+1)}>0,\tag{$1.12$}\\ \end{align*}     (b) Set $\widehat{V}(t+\Delta)=\Psi^{-1}(U_V;p,\beta)$, where $\Psi^{-1}$ is given at (1.8)
   To realize the conditional MC method, after simulating the path, calculate: \begin{align*} E\left(S_T|V(T),\int V(t)dt\right)&=S_0 exp\left(\frac{\rho}{\epsilon}(V(T)-V(0))-\kappa(\theta T-\int_{0}^{T}V(t)dt)\right),\tag{$1.13$}\\ \sigma_{BS}&=\left((1-\rho^2)\int_{0}^{T}V(t)dt\right)^{\frac{1}{2}},\tag{$1.14$}\\ \end{align*}    Then bring the forward price and volatility into the BSM option pricing model.

1.4. General discrete-time approximation schemes
  Kahl-Jackel Scheme
  Using implicit Milstein scheme to discretize the V-process and "IJK" discretization for the stock process: \begin{align*} ln\widehat{S}(t+\Delta)&=ln\widehat{S}(t)-\frac{\Delta}{4}(\widehat{V}(t+\Delta)+\widehat{V}(t))+\rho\sqrt{\widehat{V}(t)}Z_V\sqrt{\Delta}+\frac{1}{2}\left(\sqrt{\widehat{V}(t+\Delta)}+\sqrt{\widehat{V}(t)}\right)(Z_X\sqrt{\Delta}-\rho Z_V\sqrt{\Delta})+\frac{1}{4}\epsilon\rho\Delta(Z_V^2-1),\tag{$1.15$}\\ \widehat{V}(t+\Delta) &= \frac{\widehat{V}(t)+\kappa\theta\Delta+\epsilon\sqrt{\widehat{V}(t)}Z_V\sqrt{\Delta}+\frac{1}{4}\epsilon^2\Delta(Z_V^2-1)}{1+\kappa\Delta}, \tag{$1.16$} \end{align*}
  Broadie-Kaya Scheme (Exact MC)
  $V(t),V(t+\Delta)$ are sampled directly from the known conditional distribution of $V(t)$ while $lnS(t),lnS(t+\Delta)$ are from Gaussian distribution. \begin{align*} V(t+\Delta)&=V(t)+\int_{t}^{t+\Delta}\kappa(\theta-V(u))du+\epsilon \int_{t}^{t+\Delta}\sqrt{V(u)}dW_V(u) ,\tag{$1.17$}\\ lnS(t+\Delta)&=lnS(t)+\frac{\rho}{\epsilon}(V(t+\Delta)-V(t)-\kappa\theta\Delta)+(\frac{\kappa\rho}{\epsilon}-\frac{1}{2})\int_{t}^{t+\Delta}V(u)du+\sqrt{1-\rho^2}\int_{t}^{t+\Delta}\sqrt{V(u)}dW(u), \tag{$1.18$} \end{align*}

2. QE Discretization Scheme for 3/2 Model¶

2.1. Notation declaration
  Under 3/2 model, the stock price and volatility can be written as: \begin{align*} &dv_t=\kappa v_t(\theta-v_t)dt+\nu v_t^{3/2}dZ_t,\tag{$2.1$}\\ &\frac{dS_t}{S_t}=\sigma_t dW_t=\sigma_t(\rho dZ_t+\rho_{*}dX_t),\tag{$2.2$}\\ \end{align*}   where $\rho_{*}=\sqrt{1-\rho^2},v_t=\sigma_t^2$
2.2. Derive Conditional MC for 3/2 model from Heston model
  By changing the variable $x_t=1/v_t$, we obtain: $$dx_t=-\frac{dv_t}{v_t^2}+\frac{(dv_t)^2}{v_t^3}=(\kappa+\nu^2-\kappa\theta x_t)dt-\nu \sqrt{x_t}dZ_t,\tag{$2.3$}$$   which is equivalent to Heston model with new parameters: $$\nu^{'}=-\nu,\quad\kappa^{'}=\kappa \theta,\quad\text{and} \quad\theta^{'}=(\kappa+\nu^2)/(\kappa\theta)$$   Therefore, we can use QE Scheme to discretize $x_t$.
  $S_T$ can be calculated by: \begin{align*} dln(S_t)&=\left(\frac{\kappa+\nu^2/2}{x_t}-\kappa\theta\right)dt-\frac{\nu}{\sqrt{x_t}}dZ_t,\tag{$2.4$}\\ \int_{0}^{T}\frac{1}{\sqrt{x_t}}dZ_t&=\frac{1}{\nu}\left(ln\left(\frac{x_0}{x_T}\right)+(\kappa+\frac{\nu^2}{2})V_T-\kappa\theta T\right),\tag{$2.5$}\\ ln\left(\frac{S_T}{S_0}\right)&=\frac{\rho}{\nu}-\kappa\left(T\theta-\left(1+\frac{\nu^2}{2\kappa}V_T\right)\right)-\frac{1}{2}V_T+\rho_{*}\sqrt{V_T}U \end{align*}   where $V_T=\int_{0}^{T}v_tdt$ and $U \sim N(0,1)$
  Then we calculate: \begin{align*} E\left(S_T|v_T,V_T\right)&=S_0exp\left(\frac{\rho}{\nu}\left(ln(\frac{v_T}{v_0})-\kappa\left(T\theta-(1+\frac{\nu^2}{2\kappa})V_T)\right)\right)-\frac{1}{2}\rho^2V_T\right),\tag{$2.6$}\\ \sigma_{BS}&=\sqrt{(1-\rho^2)V_T/T},\tag{$2.7$} \end{align*}    Finally, bring the forward price and volatility into the BSM option pricing model.

3. Conclusions¶

3.1. Performance of CondMC-QE method for Heston model
  The CondMC-QE method for Heston model achieves almost the same accuracy as and sometimes better than QE-M scheme when delta is small, especially for at-the-money cases.

3.2. Performance of CondMC-QE method for 3/2 model
  The CondMC-QE method also works well for the 3/2 model, although in certain extreme case, the equivalent spot/forward price $E\left(S_T|v_T,V_T\right)$ brought into the BSM formula is 0 (see the warning above).
  When we use QE scheme to simulate $x_t = 1/v_t$, the volatility of $x_t$ is the same as that of of $v_t$ [see equation (2.3)], while the initial value of $x_t$ is significantly larger than that of $v_t$. Therefore, it is not common for $x_t$ to hit 0. We can safely calculate the integral $V_T=\int_{0}^{T}v_tdt=\int_{0}^{T}1/x_tdt$ in most cases.
